Thesis Research · Master's Thesis

Voronoi-Based Energy-Aware User Partitioning and DRL Resource Allocation in Dual-UAV RIS-Assisted SWIPT Networks

This thesis studies a dual-UAV RIS-assisted SWIPT network for 6G IoT coverage in remote or disaster-affected areas. The core idea is to combine offline energy-aware user partitioning with online DDPG resource allocation, so that the system can balance energy harvesting efficiency, throughput, and worst-user fairness under UAV battery constraints.

AP → dual UAV-mounted RIS → mobile users, with RAHTS-based time/space-domain SWIPT control.

📍

P1 · Battery-Aware Weighted Voronoi

Designs UAV service regions using both user density and normalized residual battery. A UAV with lower remaining energy automatically receives a smaller service region, reducing the risk of premature battery depletion and improving load balance.

🤖

P2 · DDPG-Based SWIPT Resource Control

Given the fixed P1 partitions, the DDPG agent jointly optimizes the time-switching factor τ, per-user transmit power p, RIS phase shifts θ, and RIS element switching states w over finite time slots.

⚖️

Objective · Energy + Throughput + Fairness

The primary target is energy harvesting efficiency, while average sum-throughput and worst-user throughput are used as key secondary metrics to verify communication quality and service fairness.
